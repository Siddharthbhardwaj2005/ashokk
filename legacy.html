Legacy of Shri Asoke K. Ghosh

Father of Indian Publishing

Shri Asoke K. Ghosh is regarded as the Father of Indian Publishing for his contributions to education and the publishing industry in India.

Pioneer of Low-Cost, High-Quality Books

He introduced affordable, world-class books under Eastern Economy Editions in the early 1960s.

First Publisher of Computer Science Books in India

Published India's first computer science book, Principles of Computer Programming, in 1969.

Global Collaborations

Partnered with Microsoft and MIT Press to bring international content to Indian academia.

Mentor to Indian Publishers

As a founder and leader in the Federation of Indian Publishers (FIP), he played a key role in guiding the industry.

Elevating Indian Publishing to the Global Stage

Organized the 24th IPA Congress in New Delhi in 1992 and played a major role in IPA Congress 2018.

Digital Publishing Pioneer

Introduced digital editions in 2007, aligning with the vision of Digital India.

Promoting Literacy and Social Responsibility

Through the Rimjhim Ghosh Foundation, he supports education and healthcare for underprivileged girls.

Impact on Indian Academia

His 5000+ titles have shaped curriculums in IITs, NITs, and IIMs, and contributed to syllabus reforms.

Commitment to Education

Since 1963, he has dedicated his work to making knowledge accessible for future generations.
